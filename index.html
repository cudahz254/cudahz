The Making of CUDAHZ

CUDAHZ is a dynamic and evolving music crew based in Eldoret, made up of four members: Covettoh, ZonKhliph, Mcado Vellah, and Vaspac Woo. Officially established in 2018, the group’s journey is rooted in individual growth, shared passion, and a series of key connections that brought the collective together.

The origins of CUDAHZ trace back to a rap event sponsored by UNBOUND, where Covettoh met ZonKhliph for the first time. Both were still in high school at the time, exploring their creativity and discovering their musical identities. Their connection was immediate, driven by a shared interest in music and performance. This led to the formation of their first crew, initially known as Draftin Niccurs, marking the earliest phase of what would later become CUDAHZ.

As their musical journey continued within the school environment, Covettoh later connected with Vaspac Woo during freestyle sessions. Vaspac, who was already involved in music with his own circle and had a foundation in conscious hip-hop, brought a unique perspective and energy into the growing group. The collaboration strengthened their creative direction, with the members frequently engaging in freestyle sessions and performances during school events.

After high school, the circle expanded further when Mcado Vellah was introduced to the group through a mutual friend. Like the others, Mcado Vellah had already been pursuing music independently, adding another layer of experience and individuality to the collective. With all four members now connected, the group transitioned into a more defined structure and adopted a new name—Barracudahz—reflecting their unity and evolving identity.

During this period, the crew actively developed their sound, building chemistry through consistent collaboration, practice, and shared ambition. Their efforts led to the release of tracks such as Pioneer, which introduced them to a wider local audience and marked an important step in their growth. This was followed by Knockout, a release that further expanded their reach and presence within the Eldoret music scene.

As the group matured, they underwent a final rebranding, transitioning from Barracudahz to CUDAHZ, a name that represents their refined identity and long-term vision. This change symbolized not just a shift in branding, but a step forward in their artistic journey as a unified and focused music crew.

While hip-hop remains their foundational genre, CUDAHZ has continued to evolve, embracing flexibility and experimenting with different sounds and styles. This adaptability reflects both their individual influences and their collective commitment to growth.

Today, CUDAHZ stands as a product of shared beginnings, persistence, and creative unity—four artists brought together by timing, talent, and a common purpose, continuing to build their story one release at a time.

Covettoh

Covettoh’s music journey began in 2018, starting from a foundation built behind the scenes. While still in school studying computer studies, he initially focused on sourcing instrumentals and supporting other artists rather than performing himself. However, a turning point came when a fellow artist failed to fulfill a promise of writing him a song. This moment pushed Covettoh to take control of his own creativity, leading him to begin writing and developing his own music.

His early growth was marked by participation in a local rap competition, where he quickly stood out and progressed through multiple stages. Despite having no prior experience in spoken word, he also explored this space during the competition, further expanding his artistic expression. It was during this period that he met ZonKhliph, a connection that would later contribute to the formation of a music collective.

As his presence in the school music scene grew, Covettoh continued building meaningful connections. He later linked up with Vaspac Woo and was introduced to Mcado Vellah, bringing together four artists with different styles and experiences. This marked the formation of the group initially known as Barracudahz, which later transitioned to CUDAHZ due to branding and naming considerations.

From that point, Covettoh, Mcado Vellah, ZonKhliph, and Vaspac began working closely to refine their sound, identity, and direction as a collective. Each member contributed unique strengths, shaping the group’s overall artistic foundation and growth.

Today, Covettoh stands as one of the key founding figures of CUDAHZ, with his journey reflecting leadership, adaptability, and the drive that helped bring the crew from individual beginnings into a unified musical movement.

Mcado Vellah

Mcado Vellah’s musical journey reflects a story of passion, growth, and finding purpose—elements that closely align with the foundation of CUDAHZ. From an early age, he was naturally drawn to music, inspired by the sound, energy, and expression of established artists. What began as casually rapping along to songs gradually developed into a deeper creative interest.

It was during his high school years that this interest began to take shape. Initially, writing lyrics started off as a lighthearted activity—something done for fun without serious intention. However, over time, his writing gained structure, meaning, and rhythm, qualities that resonated with those around him. This marked a turning point, as he began to recognize his potential and take music more seriously as a form of self-expression.

Before joining CUDAHZ, Mcado Vellah was already pursuing music independently, refining his style and building his identity as an artist. His journey later connected with the crew through a mutual friend—a dancer who introduced him to the group. This moment, as reflected in the CUDAHZ origin story, played a key role in bringing together individual talents into a unified collective.

Becoming part of CUDAHZ offered more than just collaboration; it introduced him to a shared vision. Surrounded by like-minded artists who were equally driven, Mcado Vellah found an environment that encouraged creativity, discipline, and growth. The collective energy within the group contributed significantly to shaping his artistry, allowing him to evolve both individually and as part of a team.

What distinguishes Mcado Vellah within CUDAHZ is his ability to blend meaningful storytelling with engaging sound. Like the crew itself, his approach to music goes beyond entertainment—it reflects real-life experiences, challenges, and aspirations, while maintaining a style that connects with a wide audience.

Today, Mcado Vellah remains an integral member of CUDAHZ, contributing to the group’s identity through his unique voice and perspective. His journey, from independent beginnings to becoming part of an established music crew in Eldoret, highlights both his personal growth and his role in the collective’s continued evolution.

Vaspac Woo

Vaspac Woo’s journey in music began during his high school years, where he had already started exploring his craft within his own small creative circle. His early sound leaned more toward emotional and conscious hip-hop, giving him a distinct artistic identity from the beginning.

During this period, he met Covettoh, and the two quickly formed a strong creative connection. They began sharing ideas, freestyling together, and building a natural musical chemistry that grew both in and outside school activities. This bond became the first step toward what would later evolve into CUDAHZ.

As their collaboration grew, Covettoh later introduced Vaspac Woo to other members of the future crew, including Mcado Vella and ZonKhliph. This connection brought together different styles and energies, gradually shaping the foundation of the group that would eventually become CUDAHZ.

While still in school, the group began to stand out through freestyle sessions and performances during school events and meetings. At times, they would even bring music equipment into the school hall during free or game periods to practice and sharpen their craft. These moments played a key role in strengthening their bond and building their shared passion for music.

As time progressed, Covettoh, Mcado Vellah, and ZonKhliph completed high school earlier, while Vaspac remained behind for a while. Despite the distance, the connection remained strong, and the members continued linking up during school holidays to freestyle, collaborate, and push their musical vision forward.

After eventually completing his studies, Vaspac Woo officially joined the rest full-time, marking a turning point where their shared passion became a unified movement. From that moment, the foundation of CUDAHZ became more solid, driven by consistency, chemistry, and a shared ambition to grow together musically.

ZonKhliph

ZonKhliph’s journey into music reflects the organic beginnings that define the story of CUDAHZ. His passion for music dates back to 2017, during his time in high school. At the time, he was an ordinary student with no clear intention of becoming an artist. However, everything changed when he mastered the delivery and style of “Rambo Kanambo” by Vybz Kartel. That moment marked a turning point, revealing a natural ability he had not previously recognized.

From then on, ZonKhliph began to take music more seriously, gradually transitioning from curiosity to commitment. Balancing academics and his growing interest in music proved challenging, but he remained determined to pursue both. His school environment played a significant role in shaping his early artistic confidence. By actively participating in school events and performances, he honed his stage presence and built a strong connection with his peers.

During this period, ZonKhliph developed a loyal and supportive fan base within the school community. The encouragement and recognition he received became a driving force, reinforcing his belief in his potential and solidifying his dedication to music. These formative experiences not only strengthened his confidence but also laid the foundation for his future growth as an artist.

After completing high school, ZonKhliph’s path aligned with that of Covettoh, a connection that would prove pivotal. Their meeting, which is central to the formation story of CUDAHZ, marked the beginning of a collaborative journey that would later evolve into one of Eldoret’s big music crews. As highlighted in the CUDAHZ origin story, this encounter bridged individual talent into a collective vision, setting the stage for the group’s eventual rise.

Today, ZonKhliph stands as a key pillar within CUDAHZ, bringing both his early influences and refined artistry into the crew’s evolving sound and identity.
